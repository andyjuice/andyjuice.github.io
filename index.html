About Me

Hey there! I'm Andy and I'm a sophomore at Duke University. I love making creative things with computer code and video cameras. My interests currently focus on the intersectionality between CS and education. At the moment, I am working with Professor Susan Rodger to help create a web app for data structure and algorithm visualization.

In my free time, I enjoy teaching Java and Python through my coding boot camp, Elevate Coding, or jumping from high places into bodies of water. Whether you are here to gauge my ability to fit into your company's work environment or just here to watch one of my videos, don't forget to download a copy of my resume as a souvenir of your visit!

CompSci Projects

The 21st century has seen computer technology interwoven into just about everything: from the shoes on our feet to our refrigerator. This is a small collection of the ways I have chosen to join the movement of tying the world together with computer science.

JAWAA: Web-based Algorithm Animation

Research Assistant

JAWAA was originally created in 1998, but rapid changes to web frameworks have rendered it unusable. This year, I am working with Duke Prof. Susan Rodger to reconstruct the web-based data visualization tool using a MEAN stack framework. (blog coming soon)

Elevate Coding Camps

Co-Founder & Head Instructor

In 2016, I started Elevate Coding Camps with my best friend Shivam. Our goal was to make computer programming exciting for younger children in our community. We have grown from a team of 2 to a team of 6 in the last 3 years and have taught over 80 students from the ages of 8-18.

Sweet Rendezvous

Web Designer

As a senior staff member at Sweet Rendezvous, I realized I could help grow business by using my technical skills to create and manage a website for the business. The website is now actively used for catering requests, questions, and job applications and has over 100 views daily.
